## List of favorite books 

<details>
  <summary>1. Classics in Total Synthesis book series
</summary>
  An insane amount of total synthesis and methodology lessons packed into these book series.
</details>
   
<details>
  <summary>2. Albert's Molecular Biology of the Cell
</summary>
  A classic introductory, but comprehensive, book on fundamentals of cell and molecular biology.
</details>

   
<details>
  <summary>3. Nestler's Molecular Neuropharmacology</summary>
  A really comprehensive textbook/reference on neurophamacology by Prof. Eric Nestler. The book, however, requires a basic understanding of cell and molecular biology, as well as pharmacology.
  [Link to Google Books](https://www.google.com/books/edition/Molecular_Neuropharmacology/YYUMZDeQNV4C?hl=en)
</details>
   
4. [*When Everyone Knows That Everyone Knows*](https://www.google.com/books/edition/When_Everyone_Knows_That_Everyone_Knows/RUqBEQAAQBAJ?hl=en&gbpv=0) by Prof. Steven Pinker.\
   This book delves into the intricacies of social dynamics from a Cognitive Science perspective.
